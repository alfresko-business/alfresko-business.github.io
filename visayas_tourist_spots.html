Tourist Spot Destinations in Visayas

Chocolate Hills


The Chocolate Hills are a geological marvel located in the Bohol province of the Philippines. This natural wonder is composed of over 1,200 perfectly symmetrical hills, which turn a chocolate brown color during the dry season. It is considered one of the country's most iconic and visited tourist destinations.

Boracay Island


Boracay is a small island in the Philippines that is known for its beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant nightlife. The island is a popular tourist destination, and is home to a variety of resorts, restaurants, and shops. Boracay is also a great place for water sports, such as swimming, snorkeling, and diving.

The Ruins, Negros Occidental


The Ruins is a beautiful and haunting remnant of a once-grand mansion built in the early 1900s by wealthy sugar baron Don Mariano Ledesma Lacson. The mansion was destroyed during World War II, but its ruins have been preserved and turned into a popular tourist destination. The Ruins is a popular spot for weddings, photoshoots, and simply enjoying the beauty of nature.

Kawasan Falls


Kawasan Falls is a set of multi-tiered waterfalls located in Badian, Cebu, Philippines. The falls are known for their clear turquoise waters and lush surroundings. Visitors can swim, relax, and explore the falls and the surrounding area. Kawasan Falls is a popular tourist destination and is a great place to experience the beauty of the Philippines.

Red Rock Hot Spring


Red Rock Hot Spring, nestled in the picturesque Philippine landscape, is a captivating oasis of relaxation. With its geothermal pools boasting soothing mineral-rich waters, this hidden gem offers rejuvenation for mind and body. Surrounded by towering red rocks and lush greenery, it's a tranquil haven for those seeking serenity amidst nature's embrace.

Casa Mariquit


Casa Mariquit is a two-story ancestral house in Iloilo City, Philippines. It was built in 1803 and is one of the oldest heritage houses in the city. The house is owned by the Javellana and Lopez kin and is well-maintained, still containing much of its original furniture and old framed photographs hanging on the walls.

Siquijor Island


Siquijor Island is a small island in the Philippines located in the Central Visayas region. It is known for its beautiful beaches, lush rainforests, and laid-back atmosphere. Siquijor is also known for its spiritual practices, and it is often referred to as the "Island of Magic." Visitors to Siquijor can enjoy a variety of activities, including swimming, sunbathing, hiking, and exploring the island's many hidden gems.

Hinagdanan Cave


Hinagdanan Cave, situated in Panglao Island, Philippines, is a natural wonder waiting to be explored. Descending into its depths reveals a mesmerizing underground world, adorned with impressive stalactites and stalagmites. Sunlight streaming through a small opening creates a breathtaking scene, illuminating the crystal-clear waters of the cave's enchanting lagoon.
