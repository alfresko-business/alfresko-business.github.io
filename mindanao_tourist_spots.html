Tourist Spot Destinations in Mindanao

Aliwagwag Falls


Aliwagwag Falls, located in the southern Philippines, is a stunning natural wonder with a series of cascading waterfalls and pools that will take your breath away. The falls are known for their unique stair-step formation, creating a mesmerizing display of rushing water against a backdrop of verdant foliage. It's a must-see destination for adventure seekers and nature enthusiasts alike.

Lake Sebu


Lake Sebu, nestled in the lush mountains of South Cotabato, Philippines, is a breathtaking gem of tranquility. Surrounded by mist-covered hills, this enchanting lake offers a serene retreat. Boasting crystal-clear waters, it is home to vibrant floating villages and rich biodiversity. Visitors can explore its beauty through boat rides, encounter indigenous T'boli culture, and savor delicious tilapia dishes.

Mount Apo


Mount Apo is the highest mountain in the Philippines, standing at 2,954 meters (9,692 feet) above sea level. It is located in the province of Davao del Sur on the island of Mindanao. Mount Apo is a popular destination for hikers and climbers, and it offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. The mountain is also home to a variety of plant and animal life, including the Philippine eagle, the largest eagle in the world.

Lake Agco


Lake Agco, nestled in the highlands of Kidapawan City, Philippines, is a geothermal wonderland known for its hot springs and tranquil ambiance. Surrounded by lush forests and rolling hills, visitors can relax in natural hot pools, enjoy the soothing waters of the sulfuric waterfall, and immerse themselves in the rejuvenating beauty of nature.

Mount Hibok-Hibok


Mount Hibok-Hibok is an active stratovolcano on Camiguin Island in the Philippines. It is one of the most active volcanoes in the country and has erupted several times in the past, most recently in 1953. The volcano is a popular hiking destination and offers stunning views of the island and surrounding seas.

Pasonanca Park


Pasonanca Park is a picturesque urban park nestled in Zamboanga City, Philippines. Known as the "Little Baguio" of the South, it features lush greenery, cascading waterfalls, and serene streams. Visitors can enjoy tranquil walks, explore the botanical garden, and relax in the park's serene and natural ambiance.

Tinuy-an Falls


Tinuy-an Falls, located in Bislig City, Philippines, is a captivating natural wonder often referred to as the "Little Niagara of the Philippines." With its multi-tiered cascades spanning 95 meters wide and 55 meters high, it offers a breathtaking sight. Visitors can swim, take bamboo raft rides, and marvel at its majestic beauty.

Monfort Bat Sanctuary


The Monfort Bat Sanctuary is home to the largest single colony of fruit bats in the world. The bats live in a cave on Samal Island, about 1 kilometer east of Davao City, Philippines. The sanctuary is a popular tourist destination, and visitors can watch the bats emerge from the cave at dusk. The bats play an important role in the ecosystem by pollinating plants and dispersing seeds.
