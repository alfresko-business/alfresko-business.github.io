Overview

El Nido is a municipality in the province of Palawan, Philippines. It is located on the northwestern tip of the island of Palawan, and is known for its beautiful beaches, limestone cliffs, and turquoise waters.

The history of El Nido dates back to the 16th century, when it was first settled by the indigenous people of Palawan. The Spanish arrived in the area in the 17th century, and El Nido became a part of the Spanish colony of the Philippines.

After the Spanish-American War in 1898, El Nido became a part of the American colony of the Philippines. The Americans built a road connecting El Nido to Puerto Princesa, the capital of Palawan.

After World War II, El Nido became a part of the independent Republic of the Philippines. In the 1970s, El Nido was declared a protected area by the Philippine government.

On June 17, 1954, Republic Act No. 1140 was approved, changing the name of the town from Bacuit to its present name, El Nido, after the edible nests of swiftlets (collocalia fuciphaga) found in the crevices of its limestone cliffs. These nests, nido in Spanish, the main ingredient for the gourmet nido soup, are being sold at approximately US$ 3,000 per kilogram.

In the 1990s, El Nido began to develop as a tourist destination. The construction of hotels and resorts led to an increase in tourism, and El Nido became a popular destination for both domestic and international tourists.

Today, El Nido is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the Philippines. It is known for its beautiful beaches, limestone cliffs, and turquoise waters. El Nido is also a popular destination for scuba diving and snorkeling.

Location

To reach El Nido, the fastest way to reach the place is by plane. There are direct flights from Manila to Puerto Princesa International Airport, which is located about 15 minutes from the town center. The flight time is about 1 hour and 20 minutes.


Once you arrive in El Nido, you can get around by tricycle, jeepney, or boat. Tricycles are the most common mode of transportation in El Nido. They are a good way to get around the town center and to the nearby beaches. Jeepneys are a good way to get to the more remote areas of El Nido. Boats are a good way to explore the surrounding islands and lagoons.
