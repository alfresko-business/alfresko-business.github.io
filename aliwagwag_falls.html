Aliwagwag Falls

Place: Cateel, Davao Oriental

Establishment Date: April 5, 2011

What Makes It Unique: Aliwagwag Falls is known for its staircase-like boulders and is considered to be the highest waterfall in the Philippines with a height of 340 meters (1,120 ft).

Overview

Aliwagwag Falls is a breathtaking natural wonder located in the province of Davao Oriental in the Philippines. It is renowned for its stunning cascades and unique tiered formation, making it one of the most captivating waterfalls in the country. Nestled amidst the lush forests of the Eastern Mindanao Mountains, this enchanting waterfall is a true testament to the beauty of nature.

The main attraction of Aliwagwag Falls is its impressive series of cascades that flow down like a grand staircase. The falls consist of more than 130 individual cascades, each varying in size and shape, creating a mesmerizing symphony of rushing water. The water tumbles down from a height of approximately 1,110 feet (338 meters), making it one of the tallest waterfalls in the Philippines.

What sets Aliwagwag Falls apart is its unique formation. Unlike traditional waterfalls that flow in a single stream, Aliwagwag splits into multiple cascades, creating a stunning pattern reminiscent of a giant natural staircase. The cascades range in size from a few feet to several meters, and they collectively form a magnificent spectacle that is a true feast for the eyes.

The lush surroundings of Aliwagwag Falls add to its allure. The falls are enveloped by a dense forest teeming with rich biodiversity. Towering trees, vibrant flora, and exotic wildlife are a common sight around the falls, creating a serene and picturesque setting. The cascades are also flanked by massive rock formations, adding a rugged beauty to the landscape.

Visitors to Aliwagwag Falls have the opportunity to get up close and personal with nature's masterpiece. There are well-maintained viewing decks and trails that allow visitors to explore the different levels of the falls and witness the cascades up close. Adventurous souls can also take a refreshing dip in the natural pools formed by the cascades, or indulge in river trekking and canyoning activities to further explore the surrounding area.

Aside from its natural beauty, Aliwagwag Falls holds cultural significance for the indigenous Mandaya people who consider it a sacred site. The Mandaya community shares their traditions and folklore, offering visitors a glimpse into their rich cultural heritage.

Aliwagwag Falls is not only a visual marvel but also a symbol of the pristine beauty of the Philippines. Its awe-inspiring cascades, lush surroundings, and cultural significance make it a must-visit destination for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and those seeking solace in the embrace of Mother Nature. A trip to Aliwagwag Falls promises an unforgettable experience that will leave visitors in awe of its grandeur and captivated by its ethereal charm.

Location

To reach Aliwagwag Falls, take an airplane bound to Davao City International Airport. From the airport, you can take a bus or van to Cateel, which is the town where the falls are located. The journey takes around 3 hours.


If you are driving, you can take the Davao-Mati Highway to Cateel. The journey takes around 4 hours.


Once you reach Cateel, you will need to take a tricycle or jeepney to the Aliwagwag Falls Eco Park. The entrance fee to the park is PHP 100 per person.
