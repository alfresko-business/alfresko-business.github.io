Chocolate Hills


The Chocolate Hills are a geological marvel located in the Bohol province of the Philippines. This natural wonder is composed of over 1,200 perfectly symmetrical hills, which turn a chocolate brown color during the dry season. It is considered one of the country's most iconic and visited tourist destinations.

Banaue Rice Terraces


The Banaue Rice Terraces are an impressive feat of agricultural engineering located in the northern Philippines. These terraces are over 2,000 years old and were created by the Ifugao people, using traditional techniques to cultivate rice on steep mountain slopes. They are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular tourist attraction.

Puerto Princesa Underground River


The Puerto Princesa Underground River is a natural wonder located in Palawan, Philippines. This subterranean river runs for 8.2 kilometers and features stunning rock formations and unique cave ecosystems, including bats and swiftlets. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the world's most impressive underground rivers.

Mayon Volcano


Mayon Volcano is a majestic stratovolcano located in the Bicol Region of the Philippines. It is renowned for its perfectly symmetrical cone shape, rising to a height of 2,463 meters above sea level. Mayon is also considered one of the world's most active volcanoes, with over 50 eruptions recorded in the past 500 years. Despite its beauty, Mayon poses a significant threat to nearby communities due to its unpredictable eruptions and pyroclastic flows.

Pagsanjan Falls


Pagsanjan Falls is a stunning natural wonder located in the province of Laguna, Philippines. It features a majestic 120-foot waterfall that cascades into a turquoise blue pool surrounded by lush greenery. Visitors can experience the falls by taking a thrilling boat ride through a gorge and navigating rapids before arriving at the base of the falls. It's a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.
