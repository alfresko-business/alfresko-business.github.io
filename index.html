Chocolate Hills

The Chocolate Hills are a geological formation located in the Bohol province of the Philippines. Composed of over 1,000 conical hills, they are named for their resemblance to chocolate drops. The hills range in size from 30 to 120 meters and are covered in green grass that turns brown during the dry season, giving them their chocolate-like appearance. The origin of the hills remains a mystery, with theories ranging from coral reefs to volcanic activity. The Chocolate Hills have become a popular tourist attraction and are considered one of the natural wonders of the Philippines.

Banaue Rice Terraces

The Banaue Rice Terraces are a UNESCO World Heritage site located in the Cordillera Mountains of the Philippines. The terraces are over 2,000 years old and were carved by hand into the mountains by the indigenous Ifugao people. They cover over 10,000 square kilometers and are considered a marvel of engineering and agricultural sustainability. The terraces are still used for rice cultivation today and attract visitors from all over the world who come to marvel at their beauty and cultural significance.

Puerto Princesa Underground River

The Puerto Princesa Underground River is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the New 7 Wonders of Nature located in Palawan, Philippines. The river flows through a limestone cave system and is over 24 km long, making it one of the longest underground rivers in the world. Visitors can take a boat tour to explore the river and witness its stunning rock formations, including stalactites and stalagmites. The site is also home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including endemic species found only in the area.
