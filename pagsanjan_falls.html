Pagsanjan Falls

Place: Cavinti, Laguna

Also known as: Cavinti Falls (local name: Magdapio Falls)

What Makes It Unique: The falls can be reached by a river trip on a dugout canoe, known locally as "Shooting the Rapids." This is a thrilling experience that allows you to get up close and personal with the falls.

Overview

Pagsanjan Falls, also known as Cavinti Falls (local name: Magdapio Falls) is one of the most famous waterfalls in the Philippines. Located in the province of Laguna, the falls is one of the major tourist attractions of the region. The three-drop waterfall is reached by a river trip on dugout canoe, known locally as "Shooting the Rapids", originating from the municipality of Pagsanjan. The falls can also be reached from the top by a short hike from Cavinti.

The boat ride has been an attraction since the Spanish Colonial Era with the oldest surviving written account dating to 1894. The town of Pagsanjan lies at the confluence of two rivers, the Balanac River and the Bumbungan River (also known as the Pagsanjan River).

The history of Pagsanjan Falls dates back to the Spanish colonial era. It was during this time that the falls gained popularity among the Spanish elites as a recreational spot. The area surrounding the falls was a thriving trade hub, and the abundance of natural resources made it an attractive destination for the wealthy and influential. The falls were often depicted in paintings and writings, immortalizing its beauty.

Pagsanjan Falls played a vital role in the development of the region. In the past, the cascading waters were harnessed to power mills and factories, driving the local economy forward. This historical significance adds depth and character to the falls, making it more than just a picturesque tourist spot.

To reach the falls, visitors embark on a thrilling river adventure along the Pagsanjan River. This boat ride, known as "shooting the rapids," is an exhilarating experience that has become synonymous with Pagsanjan Falls. Skilled boatmen expertly navigate through the rapids and narrow gorges, ensuring a safe yet adrenaline-pumping journey for the passengers.

Upon reaching the falls, visitors transfer to traditional bamboo rafts called "bancas." These rafts, skillfully maneuvered by the boatmen using long bamboo poles, take visitors closer to the base of the falls. The sound of rushing water and the refreshing mist enveloping the surroundings create a serene and magical ambiance.

Behind the magnificent waterfall curtain lies the Devil's Cave, a cavernous chamber that adds an element of intrigue and adventure to the Pagsanjan Falls experience. Accompanied by experienced guides, adventurous visitors can explore this hidden gem, marveling at its natural formations and the interplay of light and shadows within.

Location

Pagsanjan Falls is located approximately 90 kilometers (56 miles) southeast of Manila, the capital city of the Philippines. The most common way to reach Pagsanjan Falls is by land.


To reach Pagsanjan Falls, from Manila, take a bus bound for Sta. Cruz, Laguna. The bus fare is around PHP 140. From Sta. Cruz, take a jeepney to Pagsanjan. The jeepney fare is around PHP 8.


If you have a private vehicle or are renting one, you can drive from Manila to Pagsanjan. Take the South Luzon Expressway (SLEX) and exit at Calamba. From there, follow the National Highway until you reach Pagsanjan town.
