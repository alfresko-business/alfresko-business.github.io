Mount Hibok-Hibok

Place: Camiguin Island, Northern Mindanao

Also known as: Catarman Volcano

What Makes It Unique: Mount Hibok-Hibok is one of the few active volcanoes in the Philippines that can be climbed. While its volcanic activity adds an element of danger, it also attracts adventure enthusiasts and nature lovers looking for a thrilling and unforgettable experience.

Overview

Mount Hibok-Hibok is a captivating natural landmark with a rich geological history. This stratovolcano holds both educational and scientific significance, attracting adventurers, researchers, and nature enthusiasts alike.

Volcanologists classify Hibok-Hibok or Catarman Volcano as a stratovolcano and dome complex with an elevation of 1,332 metres (4,370 ft) and a base diameter of 10 kilometres (6.2 mi). It has six hot springs (Ardent Spring, Tangob, Bugong, Tagdo, Naasag and Kiyab), three craters (Kanangkaan Crater, site of the 1948 eruption; Itum Crater, site of 1949 eruption, and Ilihan Crater, site of 1950 eruption).

Its adjacent volcanic edifices are Mount Vulcan, 580 metres (1,900 ft) high, northwest of Hibok-Hibok; Mount Mambajao, 1,552 metres (5,092 ft) center of Camiguin; Mount Guinsiliban 581 metres (1,906 ft) high, southernmost Camiguin; Mount Butay 679 metres (2,228 ft); and Mount Uhay]], N of Mount Guinsiliban. There are also domes and cones at Campana Hill, Minokol Hill, Tres Marias Hill, Mount Carling, Mount Tibane, and Piyakong Hill.

The volcano has four historic eruptions: 1827, 1862, 1871-1875, and 1948-1953. The last two eruptions have been from vents on the flank of the volcano and both caused fatalities. In 1871, most of the local residents fled the area after earthquakes that preceded the eruption. The number of deaths was thought to be small. In 1861, 326 deaths were caused by ashflows that reached the sea. Some people drowned while trying to flee in boats. The 1948-1953 eruption also produced lava flows and a dome.

Mount Timpoong and Hibok-Hibok form the two major landmarks within the Timpoong and Hibok-Hibok Natural Monument. The natural monument became an ASEAN Heritage Park in 2015.

The indigenous Kamigin Manobo are believed to be the original inhabitants of Mount Hibok-Hibok.

Mount Hibok-Hibok presents an exciting challenge for adventure enthusiasts and hiking enthusiasts. Scaling the volcano requires physical endurance, as hikers navigate through dense forests, steep trails, and loose rocks. The ascent to the summit typically takes around four to five hours, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes, neighboring islands, and the azure waters of the Bohol Sea. The rewarding experience of reaching the summit creates lasting memories and a sense of accomplishment for those who undertake the journey.

Location

To reach Mount Hibok-hibok, take a plane bound for Camiguin Airport. The flight duration is approximately 1.5 to 2 hours.


From the airport, you can take a tricycle or a multicab to Mambajao, which is the town nearest to Mount Hibok-Hibok. The travel time is around 15 to 20 minutes. Inform the driver that you're heading to Mambajao, which is the starting point for hiking Mount Hibok-Hibok.
